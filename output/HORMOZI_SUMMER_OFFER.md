## 2. Overall Diagnosis

**Strengths**
- Clear dual-revenue funnel: high-ticket one-time job → recurring monthly income
- Specific local targeting (Newton County cities, not "Georgia")
- Real competitive moat: bilingual crew (Spanish-speaking market is underserved)
- Concrete starting prices — no "call for pricing" friction

**Weaknesses**
- Dream outcome is still somewhat vague — "Summer Refresh" doesn't paint a picture
- Proof is thin: only 3 placeholder reviews, no before/after count
- High-ticket jobs have no guarantee — only mowing does
- No named mechanism: it's just "pressure washing" like everyone else
- Pricing ranges are wide ($197–$250, $300–$500) — creates uncertainty
- No scarcity or urgency element on the high-ticket side
- Objections are not pre-handled anywhere in the current campaign

## 3. Value Equation Analysis

### Dream Outcome
- **Score: 6/10**
- **Issues:** "Clean property" is visual but not emotionally compelling. "Summer Refresh" is a brand name, not an outcome.
- **Fixes:** Lead with the emotional outcome — pride, relief, neighbor envy. "Pull into your driveway and actually like what you see." Make the before/after vivid in copy.

## 4. Recommended Pricing Strategy

## 6. Objection Handling Statements

### Objection 1 — "It's too expensive / I can find someone cheaper"
- **Objection:** "There are guys on Facebook doing it for $80."
- **Reframe:** "The $80 Facebook guy uses the same $200 pressure washer you can rent at Home Depot. We use commercial-grade equipment, we're insured, and we've done 130+ Newton County jobs. The result looks different — and we guarantee it."
- **Proof:** Before/after photos + Google reviews + "if it doesn't look dramatically cleaner, you don't pay"

### Objection 2 — "I'll think about it"
- **Objection:** "Let me think about it and call you back."
- **Reframe:** "Totally fine. Most people say that and never get around to it — then they're still looking at the same driveway in September. What would make you feel confident enough to say yes today?"
- **Proof:** Guarantee removes the risk. "If you're not happy, we re-do it. There's nothing to lose."

### Objection 3 — "How do I know you'll show up?"
- **Objection:** "The last guy took my deposit and never came back."
- **Reframe:** "We don't take deposits. You pay after the work is done. And if we miss a scheduled mowing without notice, your next cut is free — we put that in writing."
- **Proof:** No upfront deposit policy + written missed-cut guarantee

### Objection 4 — "I don't want a contract"
- **Objection:** "I don't want to be locked in."
- **Reframe:** "No contracts — ever. Text us to cancel anytime. We earn your business every visit."
- **Proof:** State "no contracts" in the CTA of every touchpoint

### Objection 5 — "I already have someone"
